Dharma as a Universal Principle of Order

A Philosophical and Systemic Reinterpretation Beyond Religion, Morality, and Ritual

The term Dharma is frequently interpreted as synonymous with religion, morality, or cultural practice. Such interpretations restrict its conceptual scope ‚Äî stripping it of its deeper, universal significance and confining it to a single tradition.

This paper proposes an alternative philosophical framework in which Dharma is understood not as a religious system but as a universal principle of order, knowledge, balance, and functional responsibility. By examining natural law, human behaviour, education, ritual practices, ethics, and cosmology, Dharma is redefined as the sustaining logic that maintains coherence within complex systems.

This reinterpretation positions Dharma as a cross-cultural, analytical concept as relevant to contemporary philosophy, modern science, and social theory as any Western framework ‚Äî a bold decolonisation of universal thought.

"Dharma is not a religion. It is the architecture of coherence ‚Äî the logic by which natural systems, human institutions, and cosmic order maintain their integrity."
